HSL Color Model:

The HSL color model is used to define colors based on three components:

  • Hue: The color itself, represented as a degree on the color wheel (0° to 360°). In this example, 218° corresponds to blue.
  • Saturation: The intensity or purity of the color. 100% is full color, while 0% results in gray. In this example, it's 83%.
  • Lightness: The lightness of the color. 0% is black, 100% is white, and 50% is the pure color. In this example, it's 16%, so it’s a dark blue.
